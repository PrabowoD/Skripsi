\textbf{Prabowo Darmawi}, Fish Length and Weight Measurement Using the \emph{Harris-Corner Detection} Method.  
Undergraduate Thesis, Computer Science Study Program, Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences, Universitas Negeri Jakarta, May 2026.
\\
\\
The aquaculture sector in Indonesia is one of the sectors with significant economic value for Indonesian society. However, along with technological developments, this sector faces major challenges in measuring fish length and weight.
Manual measurement of fish length and weight is time-consuming, inefficient, and may affect fish quality.
In this study, the author applies the \emph{Harris-Corner Detection} method to measure fish length and weight based on digital image processing.

The first stage used in measuring fish length and weight is \emph{pre-processing} to prepare the fish images for further analysis, followed by \emph{Harris-Corner Detection} to detect corner points on the fish images, and finally the use of a \emph{Bounding-Box} method to calculate fish length, along with a simple derivation of the \emph{Length-Weight Relationship} to estimate fish weight.
The result of this study is an algorithm capable of calculating fish length and weight based on image input.
\\
\\
\textbf{Keywords}: Segmentation, Harris-Corner Detection, Bounding-Box, Length-Weight Relationship, Fish.
